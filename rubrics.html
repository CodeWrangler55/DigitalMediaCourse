Assessment

Reusable Rubrics

The rubrics emphasize design intent, technical execution, revision, critique, and professional presentation.

Design Communication

Clear audience, message, hierarchy, composition, typography, color, and consistency.

Excellent: Design choices support the message and show intentional control.

Technical Execution

Appropriate resolution, clean files, export settings, readability, alignment, and production quality.

Excellent: Final files are polished and prepared for the required output.

Process and Revision

Research, sketches, drafts, critique response, iteration, and documentation of decisions.

Excellent: Revision visibly improves the work and is supported by clear reasoning.

Critique Participation

Specific feedback, design vocabulary, professionalism, and openness to revision.

Excellent: Feedback identifies concrete strengths, problems, and next actions.

Accessibility and Usability

Legibility, contrast, captions, alt text planning, navigation clarity, and inclusive communication.

Excellent: Work is usable by a broad audience without weakening the design.

Presentation

Organized case study, visual polish, confident explanation, and connection between problem and solution.

Excellent: The student can explain what was made, why it works, and how it improved.
