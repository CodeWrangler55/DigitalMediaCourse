Course structure

What students will practice

Design process

Students move from brief and research to sketches, drafts, critique, revision, final export, and presentation.

Studio practice

Weekly demonstrations, applied lab work, and structured critique help students build confidence and professional habits.

Portfolio outcomes

Each major skill produces a finished piece students can discuss, revise, and include in a developing portfolio.

Course arc

From fundamentals to a public campaign system

Students begin with visual communication, typography, color, and critique. They then move into digital imaging, vector systems, layout, web visuals, and short promotional video before assembling a final brand campaign case study.

  1. Weeks 1-4Design foundations, typography, color, research
  2. Weeks 5-8Image editing, vector work, identity systems
  3. Weeks 9-12Layout, digital publishing, web and accessibility
  4. Weeks 13-16Short video, capstone production, presentation

Capstone

Final campaign system

Students create a complete campaign system for an organization or product, including logo, image treatment, print collateral, social graphics, landing page visual direction, and one 15-30 second video or motion social post.
